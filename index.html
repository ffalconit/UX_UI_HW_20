People who struggle with

creating new outfits to
achieve the image they
want to give to the world.

Solution:

Assist people in
maximizing their items to

`
The project

Mix&Max is an app designed to take the guesswork out of
looking your best. Mix&Max helps us mix up clothing
already in our wardrobe to create stunning new outfits
and try them on our avatar.

Role: Team leader

Duration: 3 weeks

Methods: Interviews, survey, competitor analysis,
prototyping and remote testing

Tools: Figma, Miro, and Google drive
` `
Context

Everyone wants to put their best foot forward every day,
and for many people, that means dressing to impress.
However, many of us struggle daily to find clothing
that looks great on our bodies and coordinates with each other.



Process
` `

We examined all the data and found that were two users.

Pain points
- Struggle to find styles that fit their specific body type.
- Trouble matching items.
- Choosing outfits can be time-consuming.
- People buy clothing items they never wear.
Gain points
- People dress to look good and equally dress for the weather
- Tendency to buy clothing as a treat.
- Personal tastes matter.
` `
Define

For matters of time, we decided to focus on one user and chose the type of user we identified with more limitations when selecting clothes.
` `
Ideate

We brainstormed quick options for uploading
clothing information, how to show combinations,
and how to personalize the experience.
We chose our features and established our user
flow based on these ideas.
`
Prototype
`
Usability tests

Key learnings from remotly user tests:
  • Clarification needed with the scanning process.
  • Confusing with some buttons layout.
  • Way to go back to main screen it is needed.
  • Add indications about how to take photos.
` `
Iteration
`
Key findings

People do not want to buy thousands of new clothes;
they want to have clothes that can have multiple combinations,
and they want to make these combinations in a short time.

People are interested in an app that helps to have a
more efficient wardrobe using technologies such
as augmented reality.
